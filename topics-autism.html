Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

Go to: Autism South Africa

At Elsje Stuiver Occupational Therapy we understand not only the importance of early ASD intervention but also the impact that ASD can have on the entire family.

As no two children on the spectrum are the same, great care is taken to develop aims and targets that are specific to each individual child and family’s needs.

Therapeutic intervention is thus focussed on family involvement and education in order to generalise skills to the home environment and to ensure sustainability of the recommended programs.

In order to provide a multidisciplinary approach, Elsje Stuiver is experienced in working and communicating with other health care practitioners that might be providing additional therapeutic services to ASD clients.

Elsje Stuiver is also highly trained and experienced in addressing the following areas that are typically associated with ASD:

  • Language and communication: Although this area also requires specialised support from speech and language therapists, Elsje Stuiver's knowledge in Alternative and Augmentative Communication (AAC) will support your child’s ability to communicate.
  • Social interaction: Skills development Sensory Integration, DIR and other behavioural techniques are used to promote awareness of clients to themselves and others, appropriate social responses, behaviours in public spaces and tolerance to change.
  • Imagination and Creative play: Sensory Integration and DIR/Floortime techniques are used to assist the child in feeling calm and alert, sharing attention, engaging and relating to others, interacting, shared problem solving and creative or abstract thinking.
  • Sensory Integration difficulties: Sensory Integration techniques are used to address the child’s behaviour, emotional responses, focus and attention. This may include the implementation of a sensory diet and/or alert program or alternatively addressing the child’s motor planning and sensory processing skills.
