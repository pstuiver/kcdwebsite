Autism Spectrum Disorder
 

Autism South Africa website

At Elsje Stuiver - Occupational Therapy we understand not only the importance of early ASD intervention but also the impact that ASD can have on the entire family.

As no two children on the spectrum are the same, great care is taken to develop aims and targets that are specific to each individual child and family’s needs.

Therapeutic intervention is thus focussed on family involvement and education in order to generalise skills to the home environment and to ensure the sustainability of the recommended programs.

Elsje Stuiver is experienced in working and communicating with other health care practitioners and can provide a multi-disciplinary approach with other practitioners that might be providing additional services to ASD clients.

She is also trained and experienced in addressing the following areas that are typically associated with ASD:

  • Language and communication: Although this area also requires specialised support from speech and language therapists, Elsje Stuiver's knowledge in Alternative and Augmentative Communication (AAC) will support your child’s ability to communicate.
  • Social interaction: Sensory Integration, DIR, Skills development and other behavioural techniques are used to promote awareness of the child to themselves and to others, appropriate social responses, behaviours in public spaces and tolerance to change.
  • Imagination and Creative play: Sensory Integration and DIR & Floortime techniques are used to assist the child in feeling calm and alert, sharing attention, engaging and relating to others, interacting, shared problem solving and creative or abstract thinking.
  • Sensory Integration difficulties: Sensory Integration techniques are used to address the child’s behaviour, emotional responses, focus and attention. This may include the implementation of a Sensory Diet and/or an Alert Program or alternatively addressing the child’s motor planning and sensory processing skills.
