Occupational Therapy Assessment
 

An assessment will almost always be the first step in your child and family's Occupational Therapy journey.

An assessment provides family members, teachers and caregivers with an opportunity to share background information and concerns regarding the child's development through verbal discussion and the completion of written questionnaires.

Activities during the assessment are focussed on developmental tasks and educational games specific to each child's age and abilities.

The assessment session provides the therapist with an opportunity to interact and engage with the child in order to establish the child's areas of strength and weakness.

The assessment's outcomes provide valuable insight on how to better support your child's every day performance and guide the therapist in developing a comprehensive treatment program.

The therapist will make recommendations regarding environmental adaptation, assistive devices and equipment, behavioural strategies, additional therapy and/or medical support.

Elsje Stuiver - Occupational Therapy offers a variety of assessment options specific to your child's age, ability and areas of concern:

  • Early Years Assessment: A one hour, individual session with the therapist in the practice, during which the child will be presented with structured and unstructured play and educational activities. Non-standardised test, observations and developmental checklists are used to determine the child's performance with regards to their peers

    Parents receive a written assessment report plus feedback during a 45 minute session with the therapist, approximately 2 weeks after the assessment.

  • School Years Assessment: A two hour, individual session with the therapist in the practice, during which the child will be presented with structured and unstructured play and educational tasks. Standardised tests, observations, checklists and questionnaires are used to determine the child's performance with regards to their peers.

    Parents receive a written assessment report plus feedback during a 45 minute session with the therapist, approximately 2 weeks after the assessment.

  • Developmental screenings: Children over the age of 4, with similar needs and abilities may on occasion be screened in groups or pairs. These screenings typically take place at their school

    Parents receive a written assessment report plus feedback during a 45 minute session with the therapist, approximately 2 weeks after the assessment.
