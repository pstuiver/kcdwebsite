School Visits
 

Classrooms are often louder, brighter and busier than the individual or small group sessions at Elsje Stuiver - Occupational Therapy.

For this reason it is valuable for the therapist to perform a school visit in order to observe the child in a classroom setting as well to as establish lines of communication with the child's teachers or educational support staff.

By request, Elsje Stuiver - Occupational Therapy is able to perform school visits during weekday mornings. School visits are billed according to the time spent at the school plus a nominal cost for travel to and from the school.

During the school visit the therapist will monitor, assess and assist with:

  • The child's ability to transition between activities.
  • Attention and concentration.
  • Participation in group discussions.
  • Working independently.
  • Completing work within a set time frame.
  • Keeping track of books, stationary and clothing.
  • Organising their work space.
  • Positioning within the classroom.
  • Sitting still.
  • Listening and answering questions.
  • Independence in toileting and dressing.
