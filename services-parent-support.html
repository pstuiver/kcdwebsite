Parent support

In order to achieve maximum gains from therapy, Elsje Stuiver Occupational Therapy recognises the importance of parent, guardian and care-giver involvement in the intervention process. Emphasis is placed on helping parents understand the different areas which may boost or hamper their child’s development.

The therapist's specialised training and experience enables her to provide the family with resources, handouts, equipment and strategies to best support the child in achieving their full potential.

After assessment and before commencing the therapy process, the parents, child and therapist will be provided with an opportunity to collaborate on setting the goals for therapy.

This goal-setting process ensures that there will be realistic expectations and tangible outcomes on which to measure the success of the therapy intervention.

In addition, each therapy session incorporates 5-10 minutes of collaboration between family members and the therapist plus the therapist's recommendations with regards to home practice and repetition.

On request, Elsje Stuiver Occupational Therapy is also available for private parent meetings as well as community and school presentations on Occupational Therapy or related topics.
